The Village

The central fountain at The Village was custom-built by the same company that designed the fountains at the Bellagio in Las Vegas!

Visit Website: thevillageatmeridian.com

Roaring Springs

Roaring Springs is the largest waterpark in the entire Northwest and has been ranked among the top 10 waterparks in the U.S. by TripAdvisor!

Visit Website: roaringsprings.com

Meridian Speedway

This historic speedway opened in 1951 and was once featured in the movie Wiener Dog Nationals, a kids' film about dog racing.

Visit Website: meridianspeedway.com

Julius Kleiner Park

The park was donated by Julius M. Kleiner, a German immigrant and successful Idaho farmer, who wanted to give back to the community that supported his family.

Visit Website: julius-kleiner-park

Wahooz Fun Zone

Wahooz sits right next to Roaring Springs and was originally called Boondocks before being bought and renamed by the same owners.

Visit Website: wahoozfunzone.com

The Initial Point

The History Center’s building inside City Hall houses artifacts dating back to Meridian’s founding in 1893, including original dairy equipment, since Meridian was once a major milk hub.

Visit Website: initial-point

Eagle Island Park

Before it became a park, Eagle Island was used as a minimum-security prison farm, and inmates once grew crops and tended livestock.
